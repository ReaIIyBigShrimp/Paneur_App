NOME COGNOME
(Budapest, 1934 - Warsaw, 1990)
Professor of international political economy - Corvinus University of Budapest

Short bio

He graduated as an economist at the Karl Marx University of Economics in 1976. He was researcher in the Institute for Research on World Economy of the HAS between 1976 and 1988, then he changed to the Kopint-Datorg Research Institute. After 1991 he taught at the Foreign Trade College, the Budapest University of Economics and the University of Debrecen. From 2000 he is professor of international political economy at Central European University and Corvinus University of Budapest. He holds his PhD since 1984, he is Doctor of Sciences since 1996. He was visiting professor in Milan (Bocconi, 1991), Helsinki (1993), Frankfurt an der Oder (Europa-Universität, 1997), Berlin (Freie Universität, 1998–2000). He is member of the HAS since 2007. His main research field is comparative economics and the international economic relations. He sharply criticized the ineffectiveness of the CMEA from the early 1980s, deeply analysed the post-communist economic transition process.
